Manhattan’s Residential Eviction Rate Rebound

Eviction rates soar, almost reaching pre-pandemic levels following covid-era eviction moratorium

By Lauren Rapp

November 30, 2023

Photo credit: Huseyn Naghiyev

Residential evictions in Manhattan came to almost a complete halt during the covid-related eviction moratorium. The Tenant Safe Harbor Act, which lasted between March 7th, 2020 to January 15, 2022, allowed tenants to stay in their homes even if they were experiencing financial hardship. Almost two years later, New York City has seen an 83% increase in residential evictions.

<

Oksana Mironova, the Senior Policy Analyst at the Community Service Society of New York, points to rent increases, evictions filed during the moratorium that can now be finalized, and loopholes in rent stabilization laws as causes of the current eviction rate. Mironova says passing housing assistance programs like the Housing Access Voucher Program (HAVP) (S568/A4021) could decrease evictions.

“Rental assistance is the way to fix this, basically.”

If you are struggling with potential eviction, you can find resources at New York State’s Emergency Rental Assistance Program (ERAP) website.
